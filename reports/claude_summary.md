# Executive Summary: HumanTech Intelligence Report

## Overview
Analysis of 50,000 IT support tickets reveals clear patterns in user behaviour and system friction points, offering opportunities to reduce support burden through targeted improvements.

## Key Findings

**Top Three Issue Categories:**
1. **Memory/Access (32%)** – Password and login challenges are the leading cause of support requests
2. **Communication/Collaboration (29%)** – Teams, Outlook, and meeting tools create confusion
3. **General IT Support (26%)** – Recurring issues indicate documentation gaps

**Secondary Concerns:**
- Mental model gaps (8%) – Users lack understanding of how systems work
- Security awareness (2%) – Phishing and cyber risks need reinforcement
- Training and anxiety (2%) – Knowledge gaps and technology hesitation

## Recommended Actions

**Priority 1:** Implement passwordless authentication or SSO to eliminate memory-related tickets (potential 16,000 ticket reduction)

**Priority 2:** Enhance collaboration tool documentation and create visual guides for high-friction areas

**Priority 3:** Develop targeted training and self-service resources to address recurring patterns

## Strategic Value
This approach focuses on **system and process improvement** rather than user blame, directly improving employee experience while reducing IT support costs.
